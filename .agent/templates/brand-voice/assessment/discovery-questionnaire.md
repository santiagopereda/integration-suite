# Brand Voice Discovery Questionnaire

Guided interview structure for defining or documenting a brand's voice. Adapt questions based on context (new brand vs established, B2B vs B2C).

## Section 1: Brand Foundation

- What does your brand do in one sentence?
- Who is your primary audience? (Demographics, psychographics, technical level)
- What problem do you solve for them?
- What is your brand's mission or purpose beyond profit?
- How would you describe your brand's personality if it were a person?

## Section 2: Current Voice Assessment

- Do you have any existing voice/tone guidelines? (If yes, load and review)
- What 3 words best describe how you want to sound?
- What 3 words describe how you do NOT want to sound?
- Share 2-3 examples of content you love (yours or others)
- Share 1-2 examples of content that feels wrong for your brand

## Section 3: Audience Relationship

- How do you want your audience to feel after reading your content?
- What is the power dynamic? (Expert-to-learner, peer-to-peer, service-to-customer)
- How formal or casual is your relationship with your audience?
- Do you use humor? If so, what kind? (Wit, wordplay, self-deprecating, none)
- How do you address your audience? (You, we, they, by role)

## Section 4: Values & Beliefs

- What are your brand's core values? (List 3-5)
- What beliefs differentiate you from competitors?
- What causes or positions does your brand take? (Or deliberately avoid)
- How important is inclusivity and accessibility in your language?
- Are there cultural or social considerations that shape your voice?

## Section 5: Tone Spectrum

- How does your tone shift between these contexts:
  - Announcing good news vs. communicating problems
  - Educating vs. selling
  - Formal communications vs. casual touchpoints
  - Internal team vs. external audience
- What is your baseline tone? (The default when no context applies)
- How much tone variation is acceptable across channels?

## Section 6: Language Preferences

- Industry jargon: embrace, translate, or avoid?
- Preferred sentence length: short/punchy, medium, long/detailed?
- Active voice vs. passive voice preference?
- Contractions: always, sometimes, never?
- Do you use specific terminology for your product/features?
- Are there words or phrases you always/never use?

## Section 7: Channel Adaptation

- What channels do you publish on? (Website, blog, social, email, docs, support)
- Which channel is most important for brand perception?
- How should voice adapt across channels? (Strict consistency vs. channel-specific flex)
- What content formats do you use? (Long-form, short-form, video scripts, technical docs)
- Are there channels where voice rules relax?

## Section 8: Competitive Differentiation

- Name 2-3 competitors. How does your voice differ from theirs?
- What voice gap exists in your industry that you could fill?
- Is there a brand outside your industry whose voice you admire?
- What makes your voice recognizable even without your logo?

## Section 9: Content Governance

- Who creates content? (Internal team, agencies, freelancers, AI-assisted)
- Who approves content before publication?
- How are voice guidelines currently shared and enforced?
- What training exists for new content creators?
- What is the biggest voice consistency challenge today?

## Section 10: Measurement & Evolution

- How do you measure content effectiveness today?
- How often should voice guidelines be reviewed? (Quarterly, biannually, annually)
- What would trigger a voice evolution? (Market shift, rebrand, new audience)
- How do you collect audience feedback on your content?
- What metrics matter most? (Engagement, comprehension, conversion, sentiment)

## Section 11: Practical Constraints

- Are there legal/compliance restrictions on language? (Regulated industry, disclaimers)
- Are there localization/internationalization requirements?
- What is your content production volume? (Posts/week, docs/month)
- Budget and resource constraints for voice implementation?
- Timeline for voice definition and rollout?
